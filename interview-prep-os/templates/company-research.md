# Company Deep Dive — {Company}

The research you do before a loop. The goal isn't trivia — it's to walk in with a **point of view on the gap this role would own.** Save completed dives to `outputs/<company-slug>/`.

## 1. The business at a glance
- **Founded / stage / funding:** …
- **Core products:** … (list each)
- **Customer:** who they serve and what job they hire the product for
- **Revenue model:** how they make money (subscription, transaction, PFOF, etc.)
- **Moat:** the durable advantage (data, brand, network, switching costs)
- **Primary risks:** what could derail them (regulatory, default, competition, churn)
- **Mission:** in their own words

## 3. Key metrics to anchor on
The 3–5 metrics that actually tell you if the business is working (see `Knowledge/Reference/metrics-frameworks.md`). For each: what it is, and what you'd hypothesize about it today.

## 4. Your strategic POV — the gap this role owns
The most important section. In 3–5 sentences:
- What's the biggest unrealized opportunity or risk the product org faces?
- What would you do about it, and how would you measure success?
- Tie it to a concrete metric. *(Tilt example: "build a credit-graduation journey — cash advance → secured card → credit card — and measure it with cross-product attach rate, which I'd hypothesize is low today because there's no free entry point to start the flywheel.")*

This POV is your highest-signal asset in the loop. Have one or two sentences of it ready for every interviewer.

The smart, specific questions this research lets you ask each interviewer.
