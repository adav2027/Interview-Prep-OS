# First 90 Days Plan — {Role} @ {Company}

A common VP/Head-of-Product interview ask. The goal is to show judgment and humility (you learn before you reorg), then a credible path to impact tied to your strategic POV (see `templates/company-research.md`). Keep it a framework, not a fixed promise.

## Operating principle
"Diagnose before I prescribe." Days 0–30 are mostly listening; 30–60 is forming and pressure-testing a plan; 60–90 is showing early wins and direction.

## Days 0–30 — Learn & build trust
- **People:** 1:1s with the team, peers (eng, design, GTM), and key execs. Understand how decisions get made today.
- **Customers:** talk to / observe real users; sit in on sales and support calls.
- **Product & data:** learn the funnel and the metrics that matter (`Knowledge/Reference/metrics-frameworks.md`); find where the data is trustworthy and where it isn't.
- **Output:** a written "what I'm hearing" synthesis — patterns, not conclusions.

## Days 30–60 — Focus & align
- Form a **point of view** on the 1–2 highest-leverage opportunities (anchored on your deep-dive POV).
- Pressure-test it with the team and stakeholders; turn skeptics into co-authors.
- Establish operating rhythm: planning cadence, decision rights, key metrics/guardrails.
- **Output:** a focused plan with explicit tradeoffs — what we'll do and what we won't.

## Days 60–90 — Show momentum
- Ship an early, visible win that proves the direction (small but real).
- Get buy-in on the larger roadmap and resourcing.
- Close the loop: what we learned, what's next.
- **Output:** a roadmap + a metric you're now driving, with a baseline.

## How to deliver it in the interview
- Lead with the operating principle (learn first), not a list of features you'll ship.
- Tie the 60–90 day focus to your strategic POV on the gap the role owns.
- Name what you'd need from the org to succeed (decision rights, access, a key hire).
