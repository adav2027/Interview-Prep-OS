# Questions-to-Ask Bank

The questions you ask are a signal — they show seniority, strategic thinking, and genuine interest. Pull 2–3 per interviewer, calibrated to their role. Tailor the bracketed parts to the company (use your `company-research.md` deep dive).

## For the CEO / founder
- "What has to be true 12 months from now for this hire to be a clear win?"
- "Where do you and the team most disagree about the product strategy right now?"
- "What's the bet the company is making that's least obvious from the outside?"
- "Is there anything about my candidacy that gives you pause?" *(strong at a final/wrap-up slot)*

## For the hiring manager
- "What does success look like in the first 90 days — and the first year?"
- "What's the biggest gap on the team this role is meant to fill?"
- "How do product decisions actually get made here today?"

## For the VP of Engineering
- "Where does the PM/eng relationship work best, and where does it create friction today?"
- "How do you think about build vs. buy as you scale?"
- "What would make you say a product leader is making your engineers faster?"

## For the VP of Design
- "How is design involved in the product process today — at the problem stage or the solution stage?"
- "Where is the quality bar exactly right, and where do you wish it were higher?"

## For the CPO / Head of Product
- "How is the product org structured, and how might it evolve?"
- "What's the operating rhythm — planning, reviews, decision rights?"

## For Sales / GTM / COO
- "Where does product help the commercial motion most today — and where is it a bottleneck?"
- "How do product and revenue align on priorities and guardrails?"

## Strategic / POV-driven (any senior interviewer)
- "I have a hypothesis that [your strategic POV from the deep dive] — how does that land with how you see it?"
- "What's the constraint that, if removed, would unlock the most growth?"

## Avoid
- Anything answerable from the website or press.
- Comp/logistics in early rounds (save for the recruiter).
- Generic "what's the culture like?" — make it specific.
