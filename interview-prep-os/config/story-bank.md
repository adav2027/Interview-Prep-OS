# Story Bank

> **Fictional demo data** (Jordan Rivera). Replace with your own 6–8 anchor stories. Write each in STAR form (Situation, Task, Action, Result) and keep the **Result quantified** — that's what closes the loop for a hiring committee. See `Knowledge/Reference/star-method.md`.

Tag each story with the competencies it covers (see `Knowledge/Reference/competency-rubric.md`).

## 1. Packaging overhaul turnaround
**Competencies:** Strategy · Cross-functional influence · P&L/outcomes
- **S:** Northwind's pricing was fragmented across 6 products; paid conversion stalled at 2.1% and the last overhaul attempt had died in committee.
- **T:** Get exec alignment, then design and ship a unified packaging model without tanking revenue.
- **A:** Reframed the decision as two-sided risk; embedded finance into the working group; tested on net-new users first using total gross profit (not just conversion) as the success metric.
- **R:** Conversion 2.1% → 3.4%, NRR 108% → 121%; finance lead became the program's biggest advocate.

## 2. Creating decision rights from chaos
**Competencies:** Leadership · Ambiguity · Influence without authority
- **S:** 15+ leaders each believed they were the decision-maker on the pricing program; churn was paralyzing the team.
- **T:** Establish clear decision rights without alienating stakeholders.
- **A:** Proposed a 3-person decision body to the CPO; built weekly reviews to keep others informed/consulted.
- **R:** Decision latency dropped from weeks to days; program shipped on its revised timeline.

## 3. Onboarding rebuild (activation)
**Competencies:** Execution · Customer focus · Outcomes
- **S:** New-user activation was stuck; time-to-value was 9 days.
- **T:** Rebuild onboarding to get users to value faster.
- **A:** Ran discovery, instrumented the funnel, shipped a progressive-onboarding redesign with eng and design.
- **R:** Activation +28%; time-to-value 9 days → 2.

## 4. The setback story
**Competencies:** Resilience · Judgment
- **S:** A pricing experiment showed a conversion lift but quietly degraded gross margin.
- **T:** Decide whether to roll out under EOY pressure.
- **A:** Paused, traced it to a processing-rate assumption, redesigned the guardrail metric, re-ran.
- **R:** Avoided a margin-negative launch; the guardrail became standard practice.

## 5. Influencing a skeptical exec
**Competencies:** Influence without authority · Communication
- **S:** Head of Sales feared the new model would degrade ARPU for managed accounts.
- **T:** Bring them along without watering down the strategy.
- **A:** Ran a sales beta on real accounts; used the findings to shape deal-desk guidance.
- **R:** Sales shifted from blocker to partner; beta insights improved the GTM plan.

## 6. Scaling impact beyond my team
**Competencies:** Scale · Leadership
- **S:** Experimentation was ad-hoc across product.
- **T:** Make the whole org faster at learning, not just my pod.
- **A:** Built a lightweight experiment review and a shared metrics framework; coached other PMs.
- **R:** From sporadic tests to ~40 live experiments/quarter org-wide.
