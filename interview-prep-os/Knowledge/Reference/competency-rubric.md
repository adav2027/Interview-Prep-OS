# Competency Rubric

The competencies senior/executive product interviews screen for, what a strong signal looks like, and the questions that probe each. Map your story bank against these so every competency has at least one strong story.

| Competency | What "strong" looks like | Typical probes |
|---|---|---|
| **Strategy** | Sets direction under ambiguity; frames decisions as explicit trade-offs; ties choices to business outcomes | "How did you decide what *not* to do?" "What was the bet and why?" |
| **Leadership** | Builds teams, creates clarity, develops people; code-switches between vision-setting and unblocking | "Tell me about turning around a struggling team." |
| **Cross-functional influence** | Moves people who don't report to them; turns skeptics into partners | "Align a team with no shared buy-in." "Hardest stakeholder?" |
| **P&L / outcomes** | Owns revenue/cost; quantifies impact; protects the business while driving growth | "What moved, by how much?" "How did you manage downside risk?" |
| **Execution in complexity** | Runs many moving parts without it falling apart; instruments and adapts | "A program that could have fallen apart — what did you do?" |
| **Ambiguity** | Creates structure where none exists; makes the call with incomplete data | "No clear roadmap — how did you proceed?" |
| **Customer focus** | Grounds decisions in real user needs; closes the discovery loop | "A decision you changed based on a customer signal." |
| **Resilience / judgment** | Handles setbacks; owns misses; shows what they learned | "A decision that didn't land — what happened?" |
| **Scale** | Makes the org around them better, not just their team | "Impact beyond your immediate team." |

## How to use
1. List your 6–8 stories (see `config/story-bank.md`).
2. Put a check in each competency a story credibly covers.
3. Any empty column = a gap; find or build a story for it before the loop.
4. In the prep one-pager, assign the best story to each interviewer's likely focus.
