# Metrics Frameworks

Product-leader interviews test metrics fluency: can you name the *right* metric for a goal, distinguish inputs from outputs, and reason about the full funnel? Use this to prep metrics language and to fill section 3 of the company deep dive.

## Inputs vs. outputs (use this distinction constantly)
- **Output metrics** = the results you're trying to move (revenue, retention, activation rate). Lagging.
- **Input metrics** = the levers a team controls that drive the outputs (e.g., # of onboarding steps, time-to-first-value, % of users who hit the aha action). Leading.
- **Why it matters in interviews:** strong PMs set goals on outputs but manage execution on inputs. "We targeted NRR (output) but the team's weekly metric was time-to-second-product (input)."

## The funnel (works for most product/growth roles)

**Acquisition** — how users arrive
- Viral coefficient (K-factor), % organic acquisition, referral conversion, organic CAC

**Activation** — reaching the "aha moment"
- Activation rate, time-to-first-value (TTFV), feature adoption, D1/D7 retention

**Retention** — coming back
- D30/D90 retention, DAU/MAU (stickiness), churn, Net Revenue Retention (NRR > 100% = expansion beats churn)

**Expansion** — growing the relationship (core of monetization)
- Free→paid conversion, cross-product attach rate, time-to-second-product, ARPU, Product Qualified Leads (PQLs)

**Referral** — users spreading it
- NPS, referral rate, referral program ROI

## Business-level health
- LTV:CAC (≥ 3:1 is healthy), CAC payback period, organic growth rate, expansion MRR

## How to use in a case or interview
1. Anchor on the **one output metric** the goal is really about.
2. Name the **input metrics** the team would move week to week.
3. Beware the **vanity metric** — pick the measure that captures the real business outcome (e.g., total gross profit, not just signups; cross-product attach, not just installs).
4. State a **hypothesis**: "I'd expect attach rate to be low today because there's no free entry point."

*(The expansion metrics above — cross-product attach rate and time-to-second-product — are the ones to reach for in any "graduation journey" or multi-product story.)*
