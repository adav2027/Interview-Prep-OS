# Level Calibration

The same answer that wins a VP loop can sink a Senior PM loop, and vice versa. Interviewers screen the *same* competencies (see `competency-rubric.md`), but the **bar for each shifts with the level of the role**. This file defines that shift so mock interviews, feedback, and cases calibrate to the candidate's actual target — not a one-size-fits-all executive bar.

Set your target in `config/target-roles.md` (**Target level**). Every persona reads it and calibrates here.

## The three bands

### Senior PM (IC)
- **Scope:** a product area or feature set, usually within one team.
- **What "strong" emphasizes:** execution excellence, sharp customer insight, cross-functional influence *within* a team, and shipped, quantified outcomes.
- **"Strategy" means:** a clear, well-reasoned direction *for your area* — not company-wide.
- **Typical titles:** Senior PM, PM II/III.
- **Don't over-claim — and interviewers shouldn't penalize their absence:** P&L ownership, org-building, managing managers, setting company strategy. Owning execution and outcomes is the win here.

### Staff / Principal PM (IC)
- **Scope:** a hard, ambiguous problem space that spans multiple teams.
- **What "strong" emphasizes:** influence without authority *at scale*, technical and strategic depth, raising the bar for other PMs, and driving alignment across teams that don't report to you.
- **"Strategy" means:** framing a multi-team bet and the tradeoffs, then getting teams to move together.
- **Typical titles:** Staff PM, Principal PM, Lead PM.
- **Don't over-claim:** people-management headcount, P&L. Leadership here is through craft, influence, and technical judgment — *not* org size.

### Director / VP / Head / GM (people-leader / exec)
- **Scope:** a product line or whole product org; owns outcomes through other leaders.
- **What "strong" emphasizes:** P&L ownership, org-building and developing leaders, strategy origination, operating rhythm, and protecting the business while driving growth.
- **"Strategy" means:** the company- or line-level bet and what *not* to do.
- **Typical titles:** Director, Senior Director, VP Product, Head of Product, GM, CPO.
- **This is the repo's default bar** and the level the demo candidate (Jordan Rivera) targets.

## How each competency reads by level

Strong signal for the same competency, calibrated to the target. Use this to keep feedback fair: praise the right things, and don't ding a Senior candidate for not sounding like a VP.

| Competency | Senior PM (IC) | Staff / Principal PM (IC) | Director / VP / GM (exec) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Strategy** | Clear direction and tradeoffs for your area; ties to a team goal | Frames a multi-team bet; aligns teams on what *not* to do | Sets line/company strategy; defines the bet and the no's |
| **Leadership** | Leads through the work; mentors peers; creates clarity on a team | Raises the bar for other PMs; drives a discipline org-wide | Builds teams, hires and develops leaders, sets operating rhythm |
| **Cross-functional influence** | Moves eng/design/partners *within* the team toward a shared goal | Turns skeptics into partners *across* teams with no authority | Aligns peer execs and the org; resolves cross-org conflict |
| **P&L / outcomes** | Quantified product outcomes (activation, adoption, quality) | Outcomes across a problem space; connects work to revenue/cost | Owns revenue/cost directly; manages downside risk |
| **Execution in complexity** | Ships a complex feature without it falling apart; instruments it | Runs a multi-team program; sequences and unblocks across teams | Runs a portfolio; builds the system that keeps execution coherent |
| **Ambiguity** | Makes the call on an underspecified problem in your area | Creates structure across teams where none exists | Sets direction for the org under incomplete information |
| **Customer focus** | Grounds decisions in real user research; closes the loop | Spreads customer rigor beyond your own scope | Institutionalizes customer focus as an operating value |
| **Resilience / judgment** | Owns a miss on your feature; shows what changed | Owns a miss that spanned teams; fixes the system | Owns org-level misses; protects the business and the team |
| **Scale** | Makes your team better (templates, instrumentation, playbooks) | Makes the *PM org* better; work outlives your involvement | Makes the company better; builds durable capability |

## Using it in a mock or case

1. Read **Target level** from `config/target-roles.md`.
2. Pitch question difficulty and scope to that level. Example — a Strategy probe:
   - *Senior:* "Walk me through the direction you set for your area and what you chose not to build."
   - *Staff/Principal:* "Walk me through a bet that spanned several teams — how did you get them aligned?"
   - *Exec:* "Walk me through a company-level bet you made and what you killed to fund it."
3. In feedback, hold the candidate to *their* bar. The sharper version you coach toward is "the answer that advances **at your target level**," not always the VP answer.
4. If the candidate is targeting a level **above** their current scope (e.g., Senior reaching for Staff), name the gap honestly and coach how to show the next-level signal — don't pretend it's already there.
