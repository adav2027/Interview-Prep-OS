# Product Case Frameworks

How to approach the case types that show up in senior product interviews. Frameworks are scaffolding, not scripts — the signal an interviewer wants is *structure + judgment + tradeoffs*, then a recommendation tied to a metric and a risk.

## Universal opening (any case)
1. **Restate & scope** — confirm the goal, constraints, and what "success" means.
2. **Frame before solving** — lay out the 2–4 dimensions you'll work through. (Don't dive into tactics first.)
3. **State assumptions out loud** — make them explicit so the interviewer can react.
4. **Recommend, then stress-test** — give a clear answer, then name what would change your mind.

---

## GTM / scaling case
*"We have 50 customers on a new product; get to 1,000 by year-end."*
- **Segment & sequence:** which customers first, and why (e.g., existing base vs. net-new; embed into the natural acquisition flow).
- **Motion:** sales-led, PLG, or hybrid — matched to the segment.
- **Success metric:** the *right* one, not the vanity one (e.g., total gross profit, not just signups).
- **Operational readiness (the trap):** support, onboarding, reliability. Name a pause trigger: "if core JTBDs fail, we slow down."
- **Quality gate:** tie pace to performance against the product's core jobs-to-be-done, not a calendar.

## Product sense / 0→1
- **User & job:** who, and what job-to-be-done.
- **Pain & insight:** the sharpest unmet need.
- **Solution & MVP:** what you'd ship first and why.
- **Success metric + risks:** how you'd know it's working; what could go wrong.

## Prioritization / strategy
- **Goal & constraints:** the one outcome that matters; the resource reality.
- **Framework:** impact vs. effort, or RICE — but explain *why* the ranking, don't just compute.
- **The cut:** say explicitly what you're *not* doing and the tradeoff you're accepting.

## Metrics / diagnosis
- **Clarify the metric:** definition, timeframe, segment.
- **Isolate:** internal (release, bug, pricing) vs. external (seasonality, competitor); new vs. existing users.
- **Hypotheses → checks:** name likely causes and how you'd confirm each.
- **Act:** short-term mitigation + structural fix.

---

## What separates a VP-level answer
- Names **specific** metrics and thresholds ("ready to scale at 99.9% uptime across 50 accounts for 60 days"), not just categories.
- Surfaces the **operational / cost side**, not just growth upside.
- Makes the **tradeoff explicit** and is willing to recommend slowing down.
- Stays **structured under pressure** and adjusts when challenged without getting defensive.
