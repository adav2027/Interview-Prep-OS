# Mock Interview Format

The format the `prep-interview` skill uses to run realistic mocks. Ask cold, one question at a time, then give calibrated feedback before moving on.

## Per-question format

> **[Question]:** A realistic question for the target role and competency.
>
> *(candidate answers)*
>
> **[Feedback]:** Specific observations — what landed, what didn't. Not "good job."
>
> **[What I'd want to hear]:** The sharper version — the missing outcome, tighter structure, clearer ownership.
>
> **[Follow-up]:** The real probe an interviewer would ask next.

## Principles
- **Be candid, not comfortable.** The value is honest feedback.
- **Push for the outcome.** If an answer never lands the result, say so.
- **Probe ownership.** "What was *your* role vs. the team's?"
- **Escalate when warranted.** "That answer wouldn't have advanced you — here's why."
- **Calibrate to seniority.** A Series B VP interview differs from a Fortune 500 one. Calibrate to the candidate's **target level** (`config/target-roles.md`) using `level-calibration.md`: rescope the question and hold them to *their* bar. A Strategy probe, by level: *Senior* — "the direction you set for your area"; *Staff/Principal* — "a bet that spanned several teams"; *Exec* — "a company-level bet and what you killed to fund it."

## Sample question bank (by competency)
- **Strategy:** "Walk me through a bet you made and what you chose *not* to do."
- **Cross-functional influence:** "Align a large team around a goal that lacked buy-in."
- **Leadership:** "Lead a team that had lost faith in a project."
- **P&L/outcomes:** "A time you drove a measurable revenue or cost outcome."
- **Ambiguity:** "No clear roadmap or decision-maker — how did you proceed?"
- **Resilience:** "A decision that didn't land the way you expected."
- **Scale:** "How did you make the org around you better, not just your team?"

## Debrief
After a set of questions, summarize: strongest answers, recurring weaknesses (e.g., burying the outcome), and the 2–3 things to drill before the real loop.
