# The STAR Method (executive version)

STAR = **Situation, Task, Action, Result.** At the executive level, two refinements matter most:

1. **Lead with stakes, then land the outcome.** Don't make the interviewer wait. Open with what was at risk; close with the quantified result. The most common failure mode is a detailed answer that never lands the result.
2. **Make your individual contribution unmistakable.** Interviewers constantly probe "what was *your* role vs. the team's?" Say "I" when it was you.

## Structure

- **Situation (1–2 sentences):** the context and what was at stake. *"Conversion had stalled at 2.1% and the last attempt to fix pricing died in committee."*
- **Task (1 sentence):** your specific charge. *"I owned getting exec alignment and shipping a new model without tanking revenue."*
- **Action (the bulk):** the 2–4 decisive things *you* did. Show judgment and trade-offs, not just activity.
- **Result (1–2 sentences, quantified):** what moved, by how much, and what it meant. *"Conversion went 2.1% → 3.4%, NRR 108% → 121%, and the finance lead became the program's biggest advocate."*

## Quick self-check
- Did I open with the stakes? ✅
- Is my personal role clear? ✅
- Is the result quantified and tied to the business? ✅
- Could I deliver this in ~2 minutes, then go deeper on follow-ups? ✅
